Alisa Grifo

Alisa Grifo is an artist in the field of communication and technology. For TAGAMA she adds knowledge of physical space and construction, performance and the wireless industry.

For the past 9 years she has worked as a set designer and artist in New York City and Stockholm. Prior to such she worked as a film curator in the contemporary design department at the Smithsonian/Cooper-Hewitt National Design Museum as well as an associate editor at the architecture, urban issues and design magazine "Metropolis". Long term additional projects include: apprenticeship at the Belgian theater company Need Company under the choreographer Grace Ellen Barkey, the creation of a print and paper archive of the artist Donald Judd on location in Marfa, Texas and the restoration of a Philip Johnson residence in Manhattan previously owned by the Museum of Modern Art, New York City.

She studied Art History and Business at George Washington University for her Bachelors degree and earned a Masters in Design History from Parsons School of Design.
