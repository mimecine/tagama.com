TAGAMA is an art and technology collaborative based in Stockholm and New York City.  Founded in Fall 2002,  the primary members are Peter Norrman, Marco ter Haar Romeny and Alisa Grifo.

 Since it’s inception TAGAMA has been invited to particpiate at art events in Beijing, China, (Fall 2003) and Istanbul, Turkey (Fall 2003) in addition to being asked to create an installation in Stockholm, Sweden (current).  The project developed during the applied for residency will be presented at one or more of these mentioned venues and at other future locations. 

 

For the mentioned events several new projects must be created. Below we have listed three projects, one of which we would like to create during an artists residency at the Netherlands Media Art Institute. We feel the residency will provide a good working ground ( the needed tools and technical assistance) which we require to complete the following projects. We also would very much appreciate a residency as it will enable  the three of us to be based in one location and one time zone and also allow us the concentration and attention needed.

 

 

PROPOSED PROJECTS: PEOPLE OF, UNDERDOG AND DUAL IMAGE

 

Three streaming video projects conceptualized to be first presented at the Beijing 2003 event which TAGAMA has been invited.

 

The Beijing event is constructed to encourage artistic exchange between Sweden and China.  It is funded by the Swedish Institute and supported by the Swedish Embassy in Beijing.

 

 The goal of these projects is to reach as many people in Stockholm and Beijing as possible and to capture an unique, neutral and automated documentation of people in both places. Video or image streaming is essential to the projects for the reason we wish to offer a real-time window into each country.  

 

PROJECT DESCRIPTION #1: PEOPLE OF

 

PEOPLE OF will document the people and landscape of each city to present similarities and differences.  The project uses acquired clips of video from both locations and then processes the footage.  First, it arranges the clips in order of visual similarity and then concatenates the material to form a single moving image – all programmatically. We hope to create as graceful a moving image as one sees when watching a video or film, if such is not possible we will find a solution with still images.

 

AQUISITION

Neutrality is one of TAGAMA’s concerns.  We look to document subjects in the least biased way possible. Submissions, automated portraiture and surveillance are several ways of acquiring documentary material. Among the questions we will explore are: How is neutrality best achieved?  Which situation creates the most neutral presentation in the final product?  And in such, does neutrality create a better depiction in the end? It must be determined via experimentation which way of acquiring material supports the concept best.

 

After these questions are answered we will determine an appropriate material collection process. Possibilities include:

 

1.

Image booths will be installed in heavily trafficked areas in each city.  Each booth will have multiple web cams, the usage of which will be determined by the time of day.  At the press of a button people will capture the following: close-up facial portraits, mid-range head, shoulder and torso shots, and calf and leg shots. The material will be uploaded for processing in the next step.

 

2.

Digital material may also be submitted via the project website.

 

 

PROCESSING

Software grounded in theories of image recognition must be created or found to sort the images from both cities based on visual similarity. After the images are sorted they are placed into a chain of like images and rendered to a suitable format for streaming.  This software is preferably open source or will be released under an open source license.

 

At this point the images have been automatically edited together by the program into a cinematic stream. As much as our interest lies in the compounded stream becoming a global portrait of the people of different cities, we are also interested in the cinematic aspect of using software to create an automated, never-ending experimental web film of city images, cityscapes, people, and movement.  At first there are only 2 cities or 2 types of people to blend. As the project visits different locations and grows, a global city portrait is developed.

 

BROADCASTING

1.

The video is streamed to major newspapers in each country by using rich media banners on those papers.

2.

All streamed video will be archived on the project web site for on-demand streaming and documentation with the hope of stimulating comparative cross-culture discussions.

3.

Live feed and still images will make up an installation at a TAGAMA exhibition space at each event.

4.

Live-feed projection in a city center

PROJECT DESCRIPTION #2 UNDERDOG:

 

Goal: To discover an optimal and viable participant / object which can move through the city in a neutral manner and use it to document the urban landscape. The participant is yet to be determined.  We have presented the concept with dogs, one possibility.

 

ACQUISITION

Twenty dogs, ten in each city, that are wired with GPRS video/image enabled mobile phones.  (The goal is to capture video, if we find this is not possible we will stream still images which is a feature currently available.)

 

The main motivation of the project is to present a view of each city via (what we assume are) neutral participants. Given the differences in personal freedom and discipline between countries, we believe that a neutral participant is essential for such a documentary project.  There is no editor; there is no cameraman. Only a dog with a video / camera phone and a program which automatically selects footage and streams it outward to the people.

 

Of course the “dogs eye view” may not be so interesting, we need to run tests to discover the most successful and neutral source. Therefore we would like to test the concept on the following:

-         Dogs

-         Bicycles

-         Etc.    

 

PROCESSING

A clip is selected by software based on high-movement (amount of change between frames). All clips are archived.

 

BROADCASTING

 

1.

As PEOPLE OF, this project broadcasts the selected streams onto banners in major newspapers as well as presenting streams on the project website.

 

2.

All streamed video will be archived on the project website for on-demand streaming and documentation with the hope of stimulating comparative cross-culture discussions. The entire process will be documented via still photography and video. Images of the dogs and owners will be presented with corresponding video.

3.

 A live feed and such images will form an installation at a TAGAMA exhibition space at each event.

PROJECT DESCRIPTION #3: DUAL IMAGE:

 

ACQUISITION & BROADCASTING

Static cameras will be positioned within public spaces in the cities registering daily human activity, like people on a sidewalk, shot from the side, walking through the frame, or people sitting on city benches waiting for subway or other public transportation. This material will be streamed in real-time and will be the basis for video displays presented in public spaces in each city. The wish is to create an illusion of the cities existing simultaneously, side by side.

The real-time video stream will be shown on public video displays positioned in a city environment similar to where it was captured. For example, a transparent life size display next to a busy sidewalk in Stockholm will show passerbys in Beijing, thus creating the illusion of people walking side by side either in real time, or with a slight time lag.  A projection of a man sitting down, waiting for the bus in Stockholm can be projected onto a bench in Beijing.  Again, giving the illusion of the inhabitants of another city existing next to you. In turn bridging the gap of time and space while bringing each culture physically closer to each other.

 

The positioning of cameras/webcams within public spaces will be dealt with as openly as possible, thus inviting people to understand the scope of the project, while not trying to force surveillance into the spaces. We understand the legal and cultural issues involved and need to explore this further during the process of creating DUAL IMAGE.

 

TECHNICAL AND ARTISTIC ISSUES TO EXPLORE:

Real-time streaming technology and related image quality, cameras and speed issues.

Possible legal barriers regarding real-time media such as streaming.

Issues regarding publicly located computer / video display technology for public space/presentation.

Exploration and experimentation of how a ”projected visual illusion” would best work, conceptually, technically, and aesthetically.

Artistic intrusion into public space.

Conceptual issues like time lag, and adding a layer of pre-recorded /manipulated video to the real-time mix to create a dialogue about time and space.

WHO WE ARE / TAGAMA:

 

TAGAMA is an art and technology collaborative based in Stockholm and New York City.  The primary members are Peter Norrman, Marco ter Haar Romeny and Alisa Grifo. Other particpants are invited to projects as expertise is required.  

 

 

CREATIVITY VIA INDIVIDUAL SUBMISSIONS +

 

THE MODIFICATION OF CONSUMER ELECTRONICS INTO CREATIVE TOOLS +

 

NEUTRALITY IN OUTPUT PROGRAMS VIA BUILT-IN AUTOMATION =

 

UNBIASED COLLABORATIVE CREATITY    (GOAL)

 

 

TAGAMA was formed to fight against the boredom of industry.  We transform everyday consumer electronics into creative tools for the people.  Our goal is to generate collaborative, often cross-cultural material in an automated manner and present our findings via installations at popular gathering points: a downtown city street corner, the center of a village, the major crossroads in a desert, etc.  We rely upon open source technology to make creative, engaging and entertaining solutions which inspire people to create collaboratively and / or show hidden aspects of technology in a creative manner.

 

 

TAGAMA works with 9 major goals / considerations in mind:

 

1.      To create solutions which encourage community collaboration and cooperative creativity.

 

2.      Automation, meaning automated output, is a key element in all software solutions.

 

3.      Re-use of computer equipment and use of open source software is a goal so that we are not limited by budgetary and licensing issues and other such problems regarding closed applications. We create systems using “outdated” equipment that allow us to achieve our goals whenever possible. 

 

4.      We give back to the open source community all software that is created or modified during our projects.

 

5.      All of our projects function on a global basis and their final outcome is site specific.  Once the project template is created, the final presentation is dictated by the technical means available in each location. Therefore the project adapts to its location and in doing so it becomes more location specific in itself.

 

6.      Solutions are created so that they can be used today, tomorrow and yesterday, in varying forms.

 

7.      Projects are installed in benign community meeting points; direct access to the people is a requirement.

 

8.      Nothing is theoretical, nothing is academic.

 

9.      The goal is to create “alleman” solutions---something that will touch everyone regardless of economic, political or social position.

 

BIOGRAPHIES:

 

Marco ter Haar Romeny: technical expert

 

Aside from his work in the creative field Marco was the CTO of a new-media agency, The Bearded Lady. Prior to which he worked for four years as a system and concept developer and at Icon Content, Icon Medialab Stockholm and Adera Stockholm

 

He developed Sweden’s first e-commerce site for WAP-phones. His experience includes development on Windows and Unix platforms with different technologies such as DHTML, Flash, ASP/JSP/CGI, XML/XSLT, Relational and Object databases.  He has a thorough knowledge of environments such as Apache, IIS, Oracle, MySQL, MSSQL, RealSystems and system administration in Unix and Windows. He is considered an expert in interface programming for the range of common browsers – NS, IE and Mozilla. His preferred programming environment is Java, in which he has also developed solutions with Java ME for wireless devices.  Currently, he primarily concentrates on application development and integration on GPL’d environments and derivatives to the GPL.

 

Prior to his work experience he was a student at Royal Institute of Technology, Stockholm, Sweden where he focused on a master’s degree in Applied Physics.

 

 

 

Alisa Grifo ter Haar Romeny: physical space and installation

 

Alisa Grifo is an artist in the field of communication and technology. For TAGAMA she adds knowledge of physical space and construction, performance and the wireless industry. 

 

For the past 9 years she has worked as a set designer and artist in New York City and Stockholm.  Prior to such she worked as a film curator in the contemporary design department at the Smithsonian/Cooper-Hewitt National Design Museum as well as an associate editor at the architecture, urban issues and design magazine ”Metropolis".  Long term additional projects include: apprenticeship at the Belgian theater company Need Company (needcompany.org) under the choreographer Grace Ellen Barkey, the creation of a print and paper archive of the artist Donald Judd on location in Marfa, Texas and the restoration of a Philip Johnson residence in Manhattan previously owned by the Museum of Modern Art, New York City. 

She studied Art History and Business at George Washington University for her Bachelors degree and earned a Masters in Design History from Parsons School of Design.

 

Peter Norrman: video and sound implimentation and installation

 

Peter Norrman is a media artist, filmmaker and video designer. He recently finished a 2-year project with the award winning cross-media company The Builders Association (New York), designing interactive video for the stage and exploring contemporary technologies in the context of architecture, performance, and the moving image.

 

He is the recipient of the prestigious NYSCA Media Production Award 2000 in

New York and received an OBIE award for his design work and development of interactive video technologies. He was also awarded an artist-in-residence at STEIM in Amsterdam in 1999. Previous collaborations include Ben Rubin, Diller&Scofidio, Andrea Fraser, 3-Legged Dog/Production Designer. His video design work has been shown at, Whitney Museum of Contemporary Art, NYC, Guggenheim Museum, NYC, St. Ann’s Warehouse, NYC, Kaaitheater-Luna, Brussels, DEAF 98, Rotterdam, Barbican Theatre, London, and The Kitchen, NYC.

 

In 1997-98 he studied at the Interactive Telecommunications Program at New York University, focusing on interactive video and real-time image manipulation. His has a background as a photographer and currently lives in both Sweden and New York.
